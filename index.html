Public Backlash

How cancel culture affects public sentiment towards musicians over time.

By Nikitha Gopal, Abigail Velasquez, Peter Wu

In today's social media age, we see trends happen every day. Every so often, a public figure or celebrity would trend on social media platforms such as Twitter over behavior that the general public would consider offensive or socially unacceptable by today’s standards. From politics to Hollywood to the music industry, all kinds of public figures have been called out and criticized for such behavior by the masses. This form of ​​modern ostracism is known as “cancel culture”, in which the masses attempt to call out and boycott an individual in response to problematic remarks or ideologies that they have expressed.

The Data

For our analysis, we focused on three different genres of music: K-Pop, Hip-Hop, and Pop. We then selected two cancelled individuals, one male and one female, and two individuals who have not been cancelled, also being one male and one female, to act as our controls for each genre.

K-Pop

Cancelled Artists
Lucas (NCT)

Follower Count: 2M
Reason for Cancellation:
Sexual Abuse Allegations

After allegations from former girlfriends of gaslighting and cheating, Lucas was called out and apologized for such behavior soon after the allegations. As of February 2022, Lucas still remains on hiatus from participating in any activities.
Giselle (Aespa)

Follower Count: 2.1M
Reason for Cancellation:
Lipsyncing the N word

Giselle was called out on Twitter for lip-syncing a racial slur from American R&B artist SZA’s track “Love Galore” in a behind-the-scenes video. Giselle publicly apologized a few days after the incident occured.
